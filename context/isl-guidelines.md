# Indian Sign Language (ISL) Guidelines

## ISL Grammar Rules
- **Syntax Order**: Indian Sign Language generally follows a **Subject-Object-Verb (SOV)** syntactic structure.
- **Omission of Auxiliary Words**: Helping verbs (e.g., 'is', 'am', 'are', 'was', 'were') and articles (e.g., 'a', 'an', 'the') are omitted.
  - *Example English*: "The cat is sleeping on the table."
  - *ISL Gloss*: `TABLE CAT SLEEP`
- **Tense Markers**: Tense is typically introduced at the beginning or end of sentences using time markers (e.g., `YESTERDAY`, `TOMORROW`, `PAST`).
- **Negation**: Negations are placed at the end of the sentence (e.g., `I GO NOT`).

## Non-Manual Markers (NMMs)
- Facial expressions and head movements are grammatical requirements in ISL:
  - **Eyebrows Raised**: Used for Yes/No questions.
  - **Eyebrows Furrowed**: Used for WH-questions (Who, What, Where, Why).
  - **Head Nod**: Affirmation.
  - **Head Shake**: Negation.

## Finger-spelling vs. Sign Gestures
- **Sign Gestures**: Full-word symbols (glosses) for common concepts (e.g., `HOME`, `SCHOOL`, `EAT`).
- **Finger-spelling**: Character-by-character spelling used for proper nouns, names, or words that do not have dedicated signs in the dictionary.
